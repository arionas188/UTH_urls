📌 BIOSECURITY RULES FOR THE ANESTHESIA PREPARATION AREA 🏥

  • 🔒Access restricted to authorized personnel: Entry is permitted only to veterinarians, students, and assistants involved in the anesthesia process.
  • 🧼Mandatory hand hygiene: Wash hands or use an antiseptic solution before and after contact with each patient.
  • 🧴Disinfection of surfaces & equipment: All tools, countertops, and surfaces must be disinfected before and after each use.
  • 🩺Use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Gloves, masks, protective gowns, and shoe covers must be used as required.
  • 🗑️Proper waste & biohazard disposal: All used gloves, syringes, bandages, and other biological waste must be discarded in designated biohazard containers.
  • 🦠Proper management of infectious disease patients: Suspected or confirmed infectious patients must be clearly labeled and handled with enhanced precautions.
  • 🚫No food or drinks in the area: The consumption of food and beverages is strictly prohibited to prevent contamination.
  • 🔄Dedicated equipment for each patient: Whenever possible, separate or sterilized equipment must be used for each animal.
  • 🧹Daily inspection & cleaning of the area: The area must be thoroughly cleaned and disinfected at the end of each day following the designated protocol.
  • 🚫DO NOT place leashes, muzzles, or other equipment on work surfaces: All pet accessories must be stored in a designated area and never placed on countertops or work surfaces to prevent contamination and the spread of pathogens.
